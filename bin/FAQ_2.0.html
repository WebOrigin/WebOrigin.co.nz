What are the disadvantages of using HTML templates?

While web templates can offer you a cheap and fast alternative, there are also a number of disadvantages of using them.

Unoriginal
One of the biggest disadvantages of using HTML templates is that your website will have an unoriginal design. The website that sold the template to you has likely sold it to hundreds of other people as well. There is no telling how many other sites will look exactly like yours will look.

Bad Coding
A lot of websites offer free and cheap HTML templates. The problem with these templates is that they are often coded by mediocre web designers. This bad coding can affect your search engine listings and make it very hard for people to find your website. Bad coding can even make your pages slower to load, affecting the visitors you do happen to get.

HTML Knowledge
Most people buy HTML templates because they don't know HTML and don't want to take the time to learn it. There is nothing wrong with that, but what happens when you want to change something about your website? If you play around with the template, you run the risk of messing something up. If you damage the layout, you won't have the knowledge to fix it. Therefore, you are basically stuck with the original template with no way to change it.

What are the differences between dynamic website and static website?

The main difference is that a dynamic website has a database while a static website does not. A dynamic web site uses programming in addition to the layout to not only allow the flow of data in and out of the site but to make meaningful relationships with the data. For example an online business directory may allow local businesses to sign up and create profiles for their businesses. Visitors to the site may then search the directory based on their needs.

Dynamic websites empower their owners with the ability to upload information on their own, preferably through password protected administrative interfaces. One does not require knowledge about HTML or websites design to update or maintain such a site.

In addition to the budget there are several other factors that will determine if you need a static or dynamic site. What purpose will your site serve? If you are a relatively small business or the services and products that your provide are few and/or simple to explain then a static website could suffice your needs. On the other hand if you have a large inventory of products and/or services then you may wish to empower your customers with the ability to search through your inventory based on their selected criteria with a dynamic website.

If you were renting a small villa on a beach in the Coromandel and you simply needed to show a few pictures, provide rates and contact info, a static site would do. If you were a real estate broker in a competitive market and you needed to update your listings frequently, a dynamic website would be your solution.

While a dynamic website typically cost more because of its complexity, it can be a more cost effective solution in the long run. A dynamic website is the most effective solution for sites with content requiring frequent updates and/or interaction with the visitors.

How do search engines work?

Search sites have different ways of gathering information on the content of web sites:

Some search sites are put together by human effort, such as Yellowpages. People visit web sites that have been submitted to the search site and then manually categorise those sites.

Other search sites are fully automated, such as Google and Yahoo. Programs called robots or spiders surf the web, cataloging their findings and adding web pages to the search siteâ€™s database. Robots and spiders do not just note the URL of a page, though. They also index all the words on the page (except for words like a, an, the, for and so on).

When someone runs a search, the search terms are compared with the indexed words. Links to whatever sites match the search terms are then shown to the person doing the search.

In addition to the words in text of your page (and sometimes in the alt text fields of images and other elements as well), search engines also index meta keywords and meta description.
